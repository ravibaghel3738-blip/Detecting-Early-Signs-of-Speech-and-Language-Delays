🏠 Home – Issue & Purpose

Speech and language development is essential for children’s communication, relationships, and learning. When children experience delays, they may struggle to express their needs, interact with others, and participate in activities.

In many early childhood settings, early signs are sometimes missed because educators and families may feel unsure about what to look for or what steps to take next. This can delay support for children who need it.

👉 Purpose of this website:

  • Helping identify early signs of delays
  • Providing simple observation strategies
  • Offering practical tools and trusted resources
  • Guiding next steps for early support

👉 Our goal is to help you move from noticing concerns → taking action early.

🔍 Early Signs of Speech & Language Delays

👶 Infants (0–12 months)

  • Limited or no babbling
  • Lack of eye contact
  • Not responding to name
  • Limited interest in interaction

🧸 Toddlers (1–2 years)

  • Not speaking first words
  • Limited vocabulary
  • Difficulty understanding simple instructions
  • Does not imitate sounds or words

👧 Children (2–3 years)

  • Difficulty combining two words
  • Unclear speech
  • Limited interaction with others
  • Difficulty following simple directions

⚠️ Watch for:

  • Loss of previously learned words
  • Lack of gestures (pointing, waving)
  • Frustration when trying to communicate

👉 Important Tip: Look for patterns over time, not just one situation.

🧠 What Educators & Parents Can Do

✔ For Educators:

  • Observe children during play, routines, and group activities
  • Look for repeated communication patterns
  • Document observations clearly (what, when, where)
  • Support communication through play and interaction
  • Use simple language and give children time to respond

✔ For Parents:

  • Talk, read, and sing with your child daily
  • Encourage turn-taking conversations
  • Respond to your child’s attempts to communicate
  • Notice any changes or concerns

👉 Important Reminder: Educators identify concerns but do not diagnose delays.

👉 Early support helps improve communication, confidence, and learning.

🤝 Working Together (Families & Educators)

Strong partnerships between educators and families are important for supporting children. When concerns are shared early, children can receive support sooner.

✔ How to communicate effectively:

  • Share observations respectfully and clearly
  • Focus on what you have noticed, not assumptions
  • Use supportive and non-judgmental language
  • Work together to explore next steps

💬 Example phrases:

  • “I’ve noticed something I’d like to share.”
  • “Let’s work together to support your child.”
  • “We can look at some helpful resources together.”
